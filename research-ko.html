Research

Marine heatwaves are prolonged periods of hotter-than-usual sea surface temperatures. My research focuses on understanding the physical drivers and cascading consequences of these climate extremes.

Atmospheric Modeling

Atmosphere-Land Feedbacks

Using advanced atmospheric modeling frameworks, I investigated how marine heatwave events feed back into the global atmosphere and dynamically modulate rainfall patterns on land.

Oceanic Modeling

Coastal Watermass Redistribution

I utilize physical oceanic models to simulate and track coastal watermass redistribution and circulation shifts during these intense marine climate anomalies.

Ecological Consequences

Marine Ecosystem Disruptions

Beyond physical mechanics, I analyze the cascading impacts of thermal extremes on marine ecosystems, focusing on critical disruptions such as coral bleaching event thresholds, dissolved oxygen supply drops, and rapid shifts in marine species distributions.
